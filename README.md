# MUIC-ICMA216-Calculus-IIIA

## Course Overview

This course covers introductory multivariable calculus and 3-dimensional analytic geometry. The main topics include vectors in 3-dimensional space, lines and planes, vector-valued functions, and the beginning of multivariable calculus such as limits, continuity, partial derivatives, the chain rule, directional derivatives, gradients, tangent planes, and maxima/minima of functions of two variables.

Although the topic list might seem daunting at first, the actual material in this course is quite light. At least in my experience, this was the easiest class I took up to this point, and the easiest class in my Year 1.

## My Experience

The course was taught by **Prof. Ruth J. Skulkhu**.

She usually posted the class materials right before class. These were usually PDF lecture notes with blank spaces for students' own notes and exercises, and then she would go through those in class. There might also be additional problems discussed in class that were not included in the given lecture notes, but from my experience, those additional problems were usually not on the exam.

The class itself was quite chill. The syllabus said there was a 5% participation score. I attended almost every class, but I never actually saw the professor take attendance, so maybe just keep track of your absences yourself to be safe.

The professor was also very active and responded very promptly. Emails and Google Classroom comments were answered almost immediately. From my experience, I do not think I ever waited more than about an hour for a reply. The exams were graded very quickly as well, so you would usually know your grade as soon as grades started being released.

There was homework for every class except revision and exam weeks. The workload was quite normal for a 2-credit course, and honestly normal for a STEM class in this college. You could usually finish each weekly assignment in about 1 to 2 hours, assuming you were not doing LaTeX typesetting for everything like I did.

Both the midterm and the final were multiple-choice, which is probably also why grading was so fast.

## Special Note: OCR/LLM to LaTeX Conversion

You do not have to typeset your homework submissions for this course. I did the last few assignments by hand first because I did not have much time during the later weeks of the trimester.

For those later assignments, I used OCR and LLM tools to automate the conversion from handwritten work into LaTeX. The process was focused on translating the handwritten content as accurately as possible into the original written form, rather than rewriting or changing the mathematics itself.

The files generated through that workflow are marked with `_ocr_llm` at the end of the filename.

## Learning Resources

You could do just fine in this course by reading the given lecture notes, as long as you did not skip too many classes.

In my experience, the lecture notes were enough.
